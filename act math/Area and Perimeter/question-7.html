A scale drawing of a proposed trapezoidal landscape design is shown in the figure below, with the given dimensions in meters. The trapezoid consists of a right triangle and a square divided into 3 isosceles right triangles. The unshaded regions will be white rock; the shaded triangular regions will be black rock. What is the area, in square meters, that will be black rock?

Approach

We can find the area of the triangle with base 14 and height 10:

$$ A = \frac{1}{2}bh $$ $$ A = \frac{1}{2}(14)(10) $$ $$ A = 70 $$

We need to subtract the smaller isosceles right triangle to get just the shaded region. The smaller isosceles right triangle is a quarter of the area of the square.

$$ A_{\text{shaded}} = 70 - \frac{1}{4}A_{\text{square}} $$ $$ A_{\text{shaded}} = 70 - \frac{1}{4}(10)^2 $$ $$ = 70-25 =\boxed{45} $$
