Susan completed 20 courses in college and her grade point average was 3.15 (A = 4.0 and B = 3.0). If all of her grades were A's or B's, and each course grade carried equal weight, what is the number of A's that Susan received?

Approach

We can calculate the weighted average by taking the sum of each value multiplied by its probability. Let's say that Susan got A's in \(x\) percent of her courses.

$$ 4.0(x) + 3.0(1-x) = 3.15 $$ $$ 4x + 3 - 3x = 3.15 $$ $$ x = 0.15 $$

She got A's in 15% of her courses.

$$ 20(0.15) = \boxed{3} $$
