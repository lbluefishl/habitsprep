To increase the mean of 7 numbers by 3, by how much would the sum of the 7 numbers have to increase?

Approach 1

We can set up a case of 7 numbers:

$$ 0, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0, 0$$

The average of these 7 numbers is 0. To increase the average to 3, one possible way is:

$$ 3, 3, 3, 3, 3, 3, 3 $$

Therefore, we must increase the sum of the 7 numbers by 21.

Approach 2

The average formula for 7 numbers is:

$$ \text{Mean} = \frac{1}{7} \sum_{i=1}^7 a_i $$ $$ 7\cdot \text{Mean} = \text{Sum} $$

If the mean needs to increase by 3, given 7 numbers:

$$ 7(\text{Mean} + 3) = \text{New Sum} $$ $$ \text{New Sum} - \text{Sum} = 7(\text{Mean}+3)-7\text{Mean} $$ $$ =7\text{Mean}+21 - 7\text{Mean}= \boxed{21} $$
