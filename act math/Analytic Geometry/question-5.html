Loto begins at his back door and walks 8 yards east, 6 yards north, 12 yards east, and 5 yards north to the barn door. About how many yards less would he walk if he could walk directly from the back door to the barn door?

Approach

We can make a quick sketch of the path:

Going directly would be the same as going the distance of the hypotenuse of the triangle formed by the path:

The hypotenuse can be found using the pythagorean theorem:

$$ a^2+b^2=c^2 $$ $$ 20^2 + 11^2 = c^2 $$ $$ 400+121=c^2 $$ $$ c=\sqrt{521} \approx 22.8 $$

The original path is:

$$ 8+6+12+5 = 31 $$

The difference:

$$ 31 - 22.8 \approx \boxed{8} $$
