In the right triangle \(\triangle{ABC}\) shown below, \(AB=0\) units and \(BC=12\) units. What is \(\sin{A}\) ?

Approach 1

Sine refers to the ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse. We can get the hypotenuse using the pythagorean theorem.

$$ a^2+b^2 = c^2 $$ $$ 9^2 + 12 ^2 = c^2 $$ $$ 81+144 = c^2 $$ $$ 225 = c^2 $$ $$ c= 15 $$ $$ \sin{A} = \frac{12}{15} =\boxed{\frac{4}{5}}$$
Approach 2

This triangle is similar to a 3-4-5 triangle. We can obtain the hypotenuse this way and then find the ratio of opposite to hypotenuse.

$$ 3(3)-3(4)-3(5) \implies 9-12-15 $$
