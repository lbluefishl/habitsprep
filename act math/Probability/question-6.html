Mr. Schulte has a fair 12-sided die, with the sides numbered from 1 through 12. On both Monday and Tuesday, Mr. Schulte will roll the die 1 time. If the side that lands faceup is numbered with a prime number, Mr. Schulte will collect the homework that dayâ€”otherwise, he will not collect the homework. What is the probability that Mr. Schulte will collect homework on both Monday and Tuesday?

(Note: 1 is NOT a prime number.)

Approach

The prime numbers are 2, 3, 5, 7, 11

Therefore, the probability of collecting homework each day is:

$$ P(\text{collect homework}) = \frac{5}{11} $$

The probability that homework will be collected both days:

$$ P(\text{collect homework on Monday}) \cdot P(\text{collect homework on Tuesday}) $$ $$ = \frac{5}{12}\cdot \frac{5}{12} = \boxed{\left(\frac{5}{12}\right)^2} $$
