For time \(t\gt 0 \), the position of a particle moving in the \(xy\)-plane is given by the parametric equations \(x=4t+t^2\) and \(y=\dfrac{1}{3t+1}\). What is the acceleration vector of a particle at time \(t=1\) ?

Approach
\(x=4t+t^2\)
$$ x'(t)=4+2t $$ $$ x''(t)=2 $$ $$ x''(1)=2 $$ $$   $$
\(y=\dfrac{1}{3t+1}\)
$$ y'(t)=\frac{-3}{(3t+1)^2} $$ $$ y''(t)= \frac{18}{(3t+1)^3} $$ $$ y''(1)=\frac{18}{64} = \frac{9}{32} $$
