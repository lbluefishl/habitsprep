A particle moves along the \(x\)-axis with its position at time \(t\) given by \(x(t)=(t-a)(t-b)\), where \(a\) and \(b\) are constants and \(a\neq b\). For which of the following values of \(t\) is the particle at rest?

Approach

The particle is at rest when the velocity is equal to 0.

$$ x(t)=(t-a)(t-b) $$ $$ x(t)=t^2-at-bt+ab $$ $$ v(t)=\frac{d}{dt}[x(t)]=2t-a-b $$

Set the velocity equation equal to 0:

$$ 0 = 2t-a-b $$ $$ a+b = 2t $$ $$ \boxed{t=\frac{a+b}{2}} $$
