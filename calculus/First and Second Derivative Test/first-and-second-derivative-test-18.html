If \(f'(x)\lt 0\) for all \(x\) and \(f''(x)\gt 0\) for all \(x\), which of the following could be a table of values of \(f\)?

Approach

We are looking for a function that is decreasing, which rules out the last two options.

Since \(f''(x)\) is positive, the value of the derivative is increasing or becoming more positive.

The table in the first option reflects a function where the derivative is decreasing or becoming more negative.

We can approximate the derivative by finding the slope between two points. For the correct option:

$$\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline x & f(x)& \approx f'(x) \\ \hline -1 & 10 &   \\ \hline 0 & 5 & -5\\ \hline 1 & 3 & -2\\ \hline \end{array} $$
