Let \(f\) be the function given by \(f(x)=\dfrac{(x-3)^3(x+4)}{(x-3)(x+2)} \). For which of the following values of \(x\) is \(f\) not continuous?

Approach

Rational functions are continous at all intervals except for \(x\) values which cause the denominator to equal \(0\).

$$ (x-3)(x+2) \neq 0 $$ $$ x\neq3, -2 $$

There is a point discontinuity at \(x=3\) and an infinite discontinuity at \(x=-2\).
