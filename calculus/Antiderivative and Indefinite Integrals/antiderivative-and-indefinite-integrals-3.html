\(\displaystyle \int x^2\cos(x^3)   dx =\)
Approach 1

Solve using \(u\)-substitution.

$$ u = x^3 $$ $$ du = 3x^2   dx $$ $$ dx = \frac{du}{3x^2} $$ $$ \int x^2\cos(\colorbox{aqua}{$x^3$})   \colorbox{yellow}{$dx$} = \int x^2\cos{\colorbox{aqua}{$(u)$}}\colorbox{yellow}{$\dfrac{du}{3x^2}$} $$ $$ = \int \frac{1}{3}\cos(u)   du $$ $$ = \frac{1}{3}\sin(u) + C $$ $$ = \boxed{\frac{1}{3}\sin(x^3)+C} $$
