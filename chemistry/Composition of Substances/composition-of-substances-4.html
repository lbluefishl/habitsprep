A 0.20 mol sample of \(\ce{MgCl2(s)}\) and a 0.10 mol sample of \(\ce{KCl(s)}\) are dissolved in water and diluted to 500 mL. What is the concentration of \(\ce{Cl-}\) in the solution?

Approach

The concentration of \(\ce{Cl-}\) is found by dividing the number of moles of \(\ce{Cl-}\) divided by the total volume.

Both \(\ce{MgCl2}\) and \(\ce{KCl}\) will dissociate into ions in solution.

\(\ce{MgCl2(s)}\)
$$ \pu{0.20 mol }\ce{MgCl2} \cdot \frac{\pu{2 mol }\ce{Cl-}}{\pu{1 mol }\ce{MgCl2}} $$ $$ = \pu{0.40 mol }\ce{Cl-} $$
\(\ce{KCl(s)}\)
$$ \pu{0.10 mol }\ce{KCl} \cdot \frac{\pu{1 mol }\ce{Cl-}}{\pu{1 mol }\ce{KCl}} $$ $$ = \pu{0.10 mol }\ce{Cl-} $$
$$ 0.40 + 0.10 = \pu{0.50 mol }\ce{Cl-} $$ $$ [\ce{Cl-}]=\frac{\pu{0.50 mol }\ce{Cl-}}{\pu{0.5 L}} = \boxed{1.0 M} $$
