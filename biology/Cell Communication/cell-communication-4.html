Approach

Cells generally communicate through chemical messengers. One such messenger specific to nerve cells are neurotransmitters which go from the axon of one nerve cell to the dendrite of another.

Electrons are not used as chemical messengers.

Transfer of a phosphate group provides energy for some forms of cell communication, but does not transmit information directly.

The flow of protons down an electrochemical gradient is used in photosynthesis and cellular respiration.
