For which of the following data sets is the mean greater than the median?

Approach

To find the median, look at the middle number (as long as numbers are arranged from least to greatest). To find the mean, we take the average of the numbers. Below are the median and mean of the number sets.

$$3, 3, 3, 3, 3, \colorbox{aqua}{$3$}, 3, 3, 3, 3, 3 $$ $$ \text{mean}=3 $$ $$0, 10, 20, 30, 40, \colorbox{aqua}{$50$}, 60, 70, 80, 90, 100 $$ $$ \text{mean}=50 $$ $$3, 9, 27, \colorbox{aqua}{$81$}, 243, 729, 2187$$ $$ \text{mean} \approx 468.7 $$ $$6, 106, 106, \colorbox{aqua}{$206$}, 206, 306, 306$$ $$ \text{mean} \approx 177.4 $$
