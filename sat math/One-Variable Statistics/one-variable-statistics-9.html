$$ \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|} \hline \text{Data set A} & 12{,}921 & 45{,}138 & 93{,}158 & 189{,}363 & 321{,}050 & 421{,}557 & 822{,}194 \\ \hline \text{Data set B} & 26{,}400 & 42{,}811 & 53{,}555 & 62{,}215 & 75{,}123 & 89{,}121 & 101{,}237 \\ \hline \end{array} $$

Which of the following is true about the standard deviations of the two data sets in the table above?

Approach

Although it is possible to plug these numbers into the calculator and obtain the exact value of the standard deviation, this will take quite a lot of time to accomplish.

Standard deviation is a measure of spread. The numbers in data set A deviate from the group because there are a lot of large and small values relative to the mean.

The values in data set B in comparison are closer to the mean of the data set. Relatively, the standard deviation of set B is lower than the standard deviation of set A.
