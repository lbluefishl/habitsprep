$$ Q=\sqrt{\frac{3dK}{2h}} $$

The formula above is used to estimate the ideal quantity, \(Q\), of items a store manager needs to order given the demand quantity, \(d\), the setup cost per order, \(K\); and the storage cost per item, \(h\). Which of the following correctly expresses the setup cost per order in terms of hte other variables?

Approach

We are tasked with solving for \(K\), the setup cost per order.

$$ Q=\sqrt{\frac{3dK}{2h}} $$ $$ Q^2=\frac{3dK}{2h} $$ $$ 2hQ^2=3dK $$ $$ \boxed{K=\frac{2hQ^2}{3d}} $$
