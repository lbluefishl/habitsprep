$$ K=\frac{5}{9}(F-32)+273$$

The conversion of temperature measured in degrees Fahrenheit, \(F\), to Kelvins, is given by the formula above. Which of the following equations could be used to convert temperatures from Kelvins to degrees Fahrenheit?

Approach
$$ K=\frac{5}{9}(F-32)+273$$ $$ K-273=\frac{5}{9}(F-32) $$

Multiplying both sides by \(\dfrac{9}{5}\)

$$ \frac{9}{5}(K-273)=F-32 $$ $$ \frac{9K-9(273)}{5}=F-32$$ $$ \frac{9K-2457}{5}=F-32 $$ $$ \boxed{F=\frac{9K-2457}{5}+32} $$
