If \(\dfrac{12}{z}=60\), what is the value of \(z\)?

Approach 1

Write both sides in fractional form and cross multiply.

$$ \frac{12}{z}=60$$ $$ \frac{12}{z}\xcancel{=}\frac{60}{1}$$ $$ 60(z)=12(1) \tag*{\tiny divide by 60 on both sides}$$ $$ z=\frac{12}{60} \tag*{\tiny simplify} $$ $$ \boxed{z=\frac{1}{5}} $$
Approach 2

Take the reciprocal of both sides. This is basically a shortcut that uses the method shown in approach 1.

$$ \frac{12}{z}=60$$ $$ \frac{z}{12}=\frac{1}{60} \tag*{\tiny multiply both sides by 12}$$ $$ \boxed{z=\frac{1}{5}}$$
