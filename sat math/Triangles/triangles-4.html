Triangles \(ABC\) and \(DEF\) are similar. How much longer than segment \(BC\) is segment \(AB\)?

Approach

The corresponding parts of similar triangles must follow the same ratio. Since \(\dfrac{DF}{AC}=\dfrac{20}{4}\), the larger triangle is 5 times the size of the smaller triangle.

Therefore,

  $$ BC = \frac{EF}{5} $$ $$ BC = \frac{21}{5} $$   $$ AB = \frac{DE}{5} $$ $$ AB= \frac{29}{5} $$
$$ AB-BC = \frac{29}{5}-\frac{21}{5} $$ $$ = \boxed{\frac{8}{5}} $$
