An analysis of a random sample of a type of battery estimated that the mean working time was 184.5 hours with a margin of error of 12.5 hours. Which of the following is the most appropriate conclusion based on this analysis?

Approach

Though several answer choices sound reasonable, we want to avoid drawing extreme conclusions. It is unlikely that every battery will have a minimum workting time of 172 hours. Similarly, it is unlikely all batteries fail before 197 hours.

A margin of error implies some error around the mean. The correct answer choice reflects this and leaves a reasonable amount of room for error (any number from 172 to 197).
