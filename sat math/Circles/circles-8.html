In a circle with center \(O\), central angle \(AOB\) has a measure of \(\dfrac{7\pi}{4} \). The area of the sector formed by central angle \(AOB\) is what fraction of the area of the circle?

Approach

Don't overthink this one. An entire circle is \(360 \degree\) or \(2\pi \text{ radians}\). Therefore, the area of the sector to the area of the entire circle is:

$$ \frac{\frac{7\pi}{4}}{2 \pi} $$ $$ = \frac{7 \pi}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{2\pi} $$ $$ = \boxed{\frac{7}{8}} $$
