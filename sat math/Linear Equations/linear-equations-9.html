The equation below gives the amount \(A\), in dollars, a plumber charges for a job that takes \(h\) hours. Bob and Todd each hired a plumber. The plumber worked 5 hours longer on Todd's job than on Bob's job. How much more did the plumber charge Todd than Bob? $$A=35h+72$$

Approach 1

The expression \(35h\) implies that for every increase in \(h\), the Amount \(A\) would increase by 35. Therefore, a difference of 5 hours would amount to:

$$35(5)=175$$
Approach 2

We can make up a scenario. For example, let's say Bob's job took 1 hour. Todd's job would therefore take 6 hours.

Todd's charge
$$A=35h+72 \text{ ; } h=6$$ $$A=35(6)+72 $$ $$A=210+72=282 $$
Bob's charge
$$ A=35h+72 \text{ ; } h=1 $$ $$A=35(1)+72 $$ $$A=107 $$

The difference in charge is:

$$ 282-107=175$$
