Mike is ordering office chairs for his company. The chairs cost $250 each, and tax is an additional 12.5% of the total cost of the computers. If he can spend no more than $10,000 on the chairs, including tax, what is the maximum number of chairs that Mike can purchase?

Approach 1

Review Percents if you have trouble with interpreting percent changes.

If we have \(c\) chairs, our inequality should look something like this:

$$ 250c(1.125) \leq 10{,}000 $$ $$ c \leq 35.\overline{5} $$ $$ c=\boxed{35} $$
Approach 2

We can try figuring out the cost of the chairs in the answer choices. We want to start with an intermediate value, such as \(36\).

$$ \text{Total Cost}=36 \cdot \$250 \cdot (1.125) $$ $$ =\$10{,}125 $$

This is over Mike's budget, so \(35\) must be the answer.
