$$ \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|} \hline x & 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 \\ \hline \\ y & \frac{12}{5} & \frac{17}{5} & \frac{22}{5} & \frac{27}{5} & \frac{32}{5} \\ \\ \hline \end{array} $$

Which of the following equations related \(y\) to \(x\) for the values in the table above?

Approach

We can test our models, but we should first figure out if the table represents a linear or an exponential model. From the table, choosing a few pairs:

 
$$ \frac{32}{5}-\frac{27}{5} $$ $$ = \frac{5}{5} $$ $$ =1 $$
 
$$ \frac{17}{5}-\frac{12}{5} $$ $$ =\frac{5}{5} $$ $$ =1 $$

This represents a rate of change of \(1\), or a slope of \(1\) in a linear equation. Only one answer choice represents this.
