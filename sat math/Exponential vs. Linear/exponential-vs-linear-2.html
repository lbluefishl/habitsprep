$$ C=130x$$ $$ C=105(1.2)^x $$

An advertiser is using one of the two models above to predict the number of clicks, \(C\), made \(x\) weeks after a web banner ad is implemented. How many more clicks are predicted by the linear model than by the exponential model 8 weeks after the advertising campaign begins?

Approach

Substitute \(x=8 \) into the two given equations.

Linear model
$$ C=130x $$ $$ C=130(8) $$ $$ C=1{,}040 $$
Exponential model
$$ C=105(1.2)^x$$ $$ C=105(1.2)^8 $$ $$ C \approx 451 $$
$$\text{Difference} = 1{,}040-451 $$ $$ = \boxed{589} $$
